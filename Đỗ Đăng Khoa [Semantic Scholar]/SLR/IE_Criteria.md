### **1\. Inclusion Criteria (IC)** 

* **IC1 (Domain Relevance):** The study must focus directly on software testing automation, specifically within acceptance testing, Behavior-Driven Development (BDD), or Gherkin scenario generation.  
* **IC2 (Methodology & Technology):** The study must utilize or evaluate Artificial Intelligence (AI), Machine Learning (ML), Natural Language Processing (NLP), or Large Language Models (LLMs) to automate test engineering tasks.  
* **IC3 (Timeframe):** The publication date must align with the active timeline of modern LLM advancements in software engineering (with primary emphasis on recent years up to 2026).  
* **IC4 (Language & Peer-review):** The document must be written in English and peer-reviewed (including journal articles, conference papers, and major preprint repositories like arXiv) to ensure academic validity.

### **2\. Exclusion Criteria (EC)**

* **EC1 (Out of Scope \- Generic Testing):** Studies that focus solely on traditional unit testing, code reasoning surveys, or test case validation without any alignment with behavior-driven development (BDD) or requirement-to-test translation.  
* **EC2 (Out of Scope \- Non-AI Frameworks):** Studies focusing exclusively on legacy or deterministic GUI/API test tools (e.g., raw Selenium, traditional Katalon) without incorporating LLMs or intelligent automation agents.  
* **EC3 (Ineligible Document Type):** Technical cookbooks, commercial user manuals, or high-level industrial guidebooks that lack a rigorous scientific research methodology or evaluation framework.  
* **EC4 (Mismatched Focus \- Structural Screening):** Papers rejected during the initial title and abstract screening phase due to complete irrelevance to the target BDD automation/LLM pipeline.
